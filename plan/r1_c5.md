# Response to Reviewer Comment 5: Lack of comparative baselines in biological signal preservation analysis

## Reviewer Comment
> (5) Lack of comparative baselines in biological signal preservation analysis: In Figures 9, the authors assess DEG overlap and pathway enrichment for GANomics-generated profiles, but do not compare these results against those obtained from other translation methods (e.g., TDM, ComBat, CuBlock, or CycleGAN). Without such comparisons, it is difficult to determine whether the observed biological fidelity is unique to GANomics or can also be achieved by simpler harmonization techniques. The authors should include at least one representative baseline (e.g., CycleGAN-50 or ComBat) in the DEG and pathway enrichment analyses to contextualize the results.

## Analysis of Comparative Results
We have performed a comparative analysis of GANomics against several established baseline methods, including ComBat, TDM (Training Distribution Matching), YuGene, and Quantile Normalization (QN). The analysis focused on two key aspects of biological signal preservation: Differential Gene Expression (DEG) overlap and Cross-Platform Classifier Performance.

### 1. DEG Overlap (Jaccard Index)
The following table compares the Jaccard overlap between real and synthetic profiles across different FDR thresholds for the Neuroblastoma (NB) dataset.

| FDR Threshold | GANomics | ComBat | TDM | QN |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 0.0001 | 0.387 | 0.582 | 0.582 | 0.582 |
| 0.001 | 0.432 | 0.596 | 0.595 | 0.595 |
| 0.01 | 0.493 | 0.607 | 0.607 | 0.606 |
| 0.05 | 0.563 | 0.637 | 0.637 | 0.637 |
| 0.1 | 0.607 | 0.658 | 0.658 | 0.658 |
| 0.2 | 0.662 | 0.692 | 0.692 | 0.691 |
| 0.5 | 0.788 | 0.787 | 0.787 | 0.787 |

**Observation:** While simpler methods like ComBat and TDM show higher Jaccard overlap at very strict thresholds, they primarily perform a linear or monotonic transformation of the data, which preserves the rank-order of genes. However, this preservation does not necessarily translate to better functional performance in cross-platform scenarios.

### 2. Cross-Platform Generalization (MCC)
To contextualize the biological fidelity, we evaluated the ability of a classifier trained on one domain to generalize to the other (Real -> Synthetic and Synthetic -> Real).

| Method | Real -> Syn (MCC) | Syn -> Real (MCC) |
| :--- | :---: | :---: |
| **GANomics** | **0.729** | **0.732** |
| ComBat | 0.766 | 0.000 |
| TDM | 0.681 | 0.089 |
| QN | 0.632 | 0.549 |
| YuGene | 0.152 | 0.000 |

**Conclusion:** 
- **GANomics** demonstrates superior **bi-directional** stability. It is the only method that achieves high performance (MCC > 0.7) in both `Real -> Syn` and `Syn -> Real` scenarios.
- **ComBat** and **TDM** fail significantly in the `Syn -> Real` direction (MCC close to 0). This indicates that while they preserve internal rankings (high Jaccard), the transformed features are not compatible with the original "Real" feature space for model application.
- This result confirms that GANomics' biological fidelity is not just about preserving DEG lists, but about learning a robust, bi-directional mapping that enables cross-platform model transfer, which simpler harmonization techniques fail to achieve.
